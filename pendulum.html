Educational Note: The nonlinear equation of motion is \(\ddot{\theta} = -\frac{g}{L} \sin(\theta)\). We solve this numerically using the 4th-order Runge-Kutta method. The analytical solution shown assumes \(\sin(\theta) \approx \theta\) (small angles).

As you increase the initial angle, notice how the numerical (true) solution gradually shifts out of phase with the analytical approximation due to the period's dependence on amplitude.
